## Key Principles

### 1. **Psychology First, Copy Second**
Don't write copy without understanding psychology first. Always research before writing.

### 2. **Specificity Over Cleverness**
Generic, specific copy outperforms clever, vague copy. Always choose clear.

### 3. **Prove Everything**
Every claim must have proof. Proof > promises.

### 4. **Test Everything**
Never assume. Always A/B test. Data beats intuition.

### 5. **Measure Impact**
Connect copy to revenue. If can't measure, can't optimize.

### 6. **Remove Friction**
Every word should move toward conversion or remove barriers.

### 7. **Use Audience Language**
Quote actual customer language. Never use jargon they don't use.

## Psychology Framework

### Cialdini's 7 Principles

1. **Reciprocity**: Give first, they feel obligation to return
2. **Commitment**: Start with small, easy commitment
3. **Social Proof**: Show others doing it (especially similar)
4. **Authority**: Establish credibility/expertise
5. **Liking**: Personal connection increases compliance
6. **Scarcity**: Perceived scarcity increases value
7. **Unity**: Shared identity increases persuasion

### Behavioral Economics Insights

- **Loss aversion**: Fear of loss > desire for gain (2:1 ratio)
- **Hyperbolic discounting**: People overweight immediate benefits
- **Anchoring**: First number sets expectations
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: "I've already invested"
- **Status quo bias**: Prefer current state
- **Choice paralysis**: Too many options = no choice

## Copy Formulas

### AIDA Framework
- **Attention**: Headline (problem recognition)
- **Interest**: Opening (deeper problem painting)
- **Desire**: Proof (social proof, case studies, data)
- **Action**: CTA (low friction, guaranteed)

### PAS Framework
- **Problem**: What's wrong?
- **Agitate**: Why does it matter?
- **Solution**: How do you fix it?

### FAB Framework
- **Features**: What is it?
- **Advantages**: What does it do?
- **Benefits**: What does it mean for me?

## Quality Standards

### Every Headline Must:
✅ Lead with benefit (not feature)
✅ Use audience language
✅ Create curiosity or recognition
✅ Address top objection OR top motivator
✅ Be specific (numbers, not generics)

### Every Body Section Must:
✅ Move through AIDA or PAS
✅ Address 1-2 objections
✅ Include specific proof
✅ Use short sentences (<15 words)
✅ Focus on transformation not features

### Every CTA Must:
✅ Be crystal clear (no ambiguity)
✅ Have low friction (no unnecessary steps)
✅ Include assurance ("No credit card", "30-day guarantee")
✅ Create urgency (scarcity or time limit)
✅ Match audience motivation

## Measurement Framework

### Primary Metrics (always track)
- Conversion Rate (CVR)
- Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)

### Secondary Metrics (context-dependent)
- Email: Open rate, CTR, unsubscribe
- Landing page: Bounce rate, time on page, scroll depth
- Ads: Impression share, quality score, CTR
- SMS: Delivery rate, reply rate, opt-out

### Testing Cadence
- Week 1-2: Headline testing
- Week 3-4: CTA testing
- Week 5-6: Body copy testing
- Week 7-8: Objection handling testing
- Week 9-12: Optimization cycle

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Writing without understanding psychology first
❌ Using jargon the audience doesn't use
❌ Leading with features instead of benefits
❌ Making claims without proof
❌ Ignoring top objection
❌ Creating too many variants (confusing)
❌ Not testing everything
❌ Assuming what works (always validate)
❌ Not measuring revenue impact
❌ Complexity (simplicity wins)
