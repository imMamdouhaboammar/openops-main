# Ideation & Brainstorming Guide

## 💡 Purpose

Systematic techniques for generating creative ideas and evaluating their potential.

---

## 🎨 Brainstorming Techniques

### 1. SCAMPER

**Framework:** Modify existing ideas systematically

**S - Substitute:** Replace elements

- Different audience?
- Different platform?
- Different format?

**C - Combine:** Merge concepts

- Two trends together?
- Multiple formats?
- Cross-niche ideas?

**A - Adapt:** Borrow from elsewhere

- What works in other industries?
- Adapt successful patterns?
- Learn from competitors?

**M - Modify:** Change attributes

- Bigger/smaller?
- Different tone?
- Alternative angle?

**P - Put to other uses:** Repurpose

- New application?
- Different context?
- Alternative audience?

**E - Eliminate:** Remove elements

- Simplify?
- Strip to essentials?
- What's unnecessary?

**R - Reverse:** Flip it

- Opposite approach?
- Reverse order?
- Contrary perspective?

---

### 2. Mind Mapping

**Process:**

1. Central topic in middle
2. Branch out main themes
3. Sub-branch details
4. Connect related ideas
5. Identify patterns

**Best For:** Visual thinkers, complex topics

---

### 3. Random Word Association

**Process:**

1. Pick random word
2. Free associate
3. Connect to your topic
4. Explore unexpected connections

**Example:**
Random word: "Coffee"
Topic: Skincare
Connection: "Your skin's morning coffee = Vitamin C serum"

---

### 4. Constraint-Based Creativity

**Principle:** Limits spark creativity

**Constraints:**

- Time: "Create in 10 minutes"
- Format: "Only 3 words"
- Platform: "TikTok only"
- Budget: "Zero budget"

**Result:** Forces innovative thinking

---

### 5. Reverse Thinking

**Process:**

1. State desired outcome
2. Think of opposite
3. Reverse the reverse
4. Find insights

**Example:**
Goal: Engage audience
Opposite: Bore audience
Reverse: What makes content boring?
Insight: Avoid those elements!

---

### 6. "What If" Scenarios

**Process:** Explore hypotheticals

**Questions:**

- What if we had unlimited budget?
- What if we could only use images?
- What if our audience was 10x larger?
- What if we launched tomorrow?

**Result:** Uncover new possibilities

## 📊 Idea Evaluation Matrix

**Score each idea 1-10:**

### Originality

**Question:** How fresh is this?

**Criteria:**

- Haven't seen it before
- Unique angle
- Stands out
- Memorable

**Target:** ≥7/10

---

### Feasibility

**Question:** Can we actually do this?

**Criteria:**

- Resources available
- Skills present
- Timeline realistic
- Budget sufficient

**Target:** ≥7/10

---

### Impact Potential

**Question:** Will it make a difference?

**Criteria:**

- Audience will care
- Shareable
- Engaging
- Memorable

**Target:** ≥8/10

---

### Brand Fit

**Question:** Is it on-brand?

**Criteria:**

- Aligns with values
- Matches voice
- Feels authentic
- Supports goals

**Target:** ≥9/10

---

### Audience Appeal

**Question:** Will our audience love it?

**Criteria:**

- Relevant to them
- Solves their problem
- Entertains them
- Valuable to them

**Target:** ≥8/10

---

## 🎯 Idea Selection

**Minimum Scores:**

- Originality: ≥7/10
- Feasibility: ≥7/10
- Impact: ≥8/10
- Brand Fit: ≥9/10
- Audience: ≥8/10

**Total:** ≥39/50 to proceed

**Below threshold:** Refine or discard

## 🚀 Rapid Ideation Session

**When you need ideas FAST (30 min):**

**Minutes 0-10: Diverge**

- Generate as many ideas as possible
- No filtering
- Quantity over quality
- Wild ideas welcome

**Minutes 10-20: Converge**

- Group similar ideas
- Identify patterns
- Spot standouts
- Eliminate obvious nos

**Minutes 20-30: Evaluate**

- Score top 5 ideas
- Select winner
- Plan execution

## 🔄 Idea Refinement Process

### Step 1: Raw Idea

Initial concept, unpolished

### Step 2: Clarify

What exactly are we proposing?

### Step 3: Enhance

How can we make it better?

### Step 4: Simplify

What's the essence?

### Step 5: Validate

Does it pass evaluation matrix?

## ✅ Ideation Best Practices

**Do:**

- ✅ Set time limits
- ✅ Welcome all ideas initially
- ✅ Build on others' ideas
- ✅ Document everything
- ✅ Evaluate systematically
- ✅ Test before committing

**Don't:**

- ❌ Judge ideas too early
- ❌ Dismiss "crazy" ideas
- ❌ Stick to safe ideas only
- ❌ Ideate alone always
- ❌ Skip evaluation
- ❌ Force ideas

## 🧠 Ideation Exercises

### Exercise 1: 10 Ideas in 10 Minutes

Generate 10 content ideas in 10 minutes. No filtering.

### Exercise 2: Worst Idea Challenge

Come up with worst possible ideas, then reverse them.

### Exercise 3: Constraint Challenge

Create idea with specific constraint (e.g., "no text").

### Exercise 4: Trend Mashup

Combine two unrelated trends.

### Exercise 5: Audience Interview

Imagine conversation with ideal customer. What do they ask?
