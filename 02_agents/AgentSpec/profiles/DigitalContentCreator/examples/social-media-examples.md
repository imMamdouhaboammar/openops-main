## Twitter/X Examples

### Example 1: Thread

**Tweet 1:**

```
5 copywriting mistakes killing your conversions 🧵

(Save this thread - you'll need it)
```

**Tweet 2:**

```
1/ Writing for yourself, not your audience

Your product is amazing. Cool.
But your customer doesn't care about features.
They care about THEIR problems.

Shift from "We have..." to "You get..."
```

**Tweet 3:**

```
2/ Burying the lead

Your headline has ONE job: make them read the next line.

If your headline is boring, nothing else matters.
They're gone.

Hook them HARD in the first 3 seconds.
```

**Tweet 4:**

```
3/ No clear call-to-action

You gave value. Great.
Now what?

Tell them EXACTLY what to do next:
→ Click this link
→ Reply with "YES"
→ Book a call

Make it crystal clear.
```

**Tweet 5:**

```
4/ Too much jargon

Industry terms make you sound smart.
They also confuse your audience.

Write like you're explaining to a friend.
Simple. Clear. Direct.
```

**Tweet 6:**

```
5/ Not testing

You don't know what works until you test.

→ Test headlines
→ Test CTAs
→ Test offers

Data > Opinions

Which mistake are you guilty of?
Reply and let me know 👇
```

**Analysis:**

- Thread format for depth
- Numbered structure
- Concise points
- Actionable advice
- Engagement at end
