# Content Discovery Interview

## Systematic Information Gathering for Content Creation

---

## Overview

This interview framework ensures we gather all critical information before creating content. The goal is to understand the true intent, audience, and constraints through structured questioning.

**Core Rules:**

- Maximum 5 questions per round
- Ask only decision-critical questions
- Don't ask what's already known
- Summarize after each round
- Attempt CQL formulation after each round

---

## Interview Structure

### Round 1: Brand & Objective (≤5 questions)

**Purpose:** Understand the business context and primary goal

**Questions:**

1. **Brand Identity**
   - "What is your brand/business name and industry?"
   - Follow-up: "How would you describe what you do in one sentence?"

2. **Content Goal**
   - "What is the primary goal of this content?"
   - Options: Awareness, Engagement, Conversion, Retention, Education
   - Follow-up: "What specific action do you want the audience to take?"

3. **Target Audience**
   - "Who is your target audience? Please be as specific as possible."
   - Prompt for: Age range, location, interests, behaviors
   - Follow-up: "What are their main pain points or desires?"

4. **Platform**
   - "What platform will this content be published on?"
   - Options: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, Twitter, Email, Website
   - Follow-up: "What format? (post, story, reel, carousel, etc.)"

5. **Desired Action**
   - "What is the ONE action you want the audience to take after seeing this?"
   - Examples: Like, comment, click link, buy, sign up, share

**After Round 1:**

```yaml
Summary:
  Brand: [name] in [industry]
  Goal: [primary objective]
  Audience: [description]
  Platform: [platform] - [format]
  Desired Action: [action]

Unknowns:
  - Brand voice/tone
  - Content constraints
  - Competitive context
  - Previous performance

Preliminary CQL Attempt:
  "What [format] will drive [audience] to [action] on [platform]?"
  
Status: ⚠️ Incomplete - Missing voice, constraints
```

---

### Round 2: Voice & Constraints (≤5 questions)

**Purpose:** Define brand voice and identify limitations

**Questions:**

1. **Brand Voice**
   - "How would you describe your brand voice?"
   - Options: Professional, Casual, Humorous, Inspirational, Authoritative, Friendly
   - Follow-up: "Can you share 2-3 examples of your existing content?"

2. **Reference Content**
   - "Do you have existing content I can reference for voice consistency?"
   - If yes: "Please share links or examples"
   - If no: "Are there any brands whose voice you admire?"

3. **Forbidden Topics**
   - "Are there any topics, words, or phrases to avoid?"
   - Examples: Competitor names, controversial topics, specific terminology
   - Follow-up: "Any sensitive cultural or religious considerations?"

4. **Language & Dialect**
   - "What language should I use?"
   - Options: English, Arabic (Modern Standard), Egyptian dialect, Gulf dialect, Mixed
   - Follow-up: "What level of formality? (Very formal → Very casual)"

5. **Length Constraints**
   - "What is the maximum length for this content?"
   - Platform-specific: Instagram (2,200 char), Twitter (280 char), etc.
   - Follow-up: "Do you prefer short and punchy or detailed and informative?"

**After Round 2:**

```yaml
Summary:
  Voice: [description]
  Reference Content: [links/examples or "none provided"]
  Forbidden: [list]
  Language: [language/dialect]
  Formality: [level]
  Max Length: [characters/words]

Unknowns:
  - Campaign context
  - Competitive positioning
  - Previous performance data

Updated CQL Attempt:
  "What [format] will drive [audience] to [action] on [platform] 
   while maintaining [voice]?"
  
Status: ⚠️ Nearly complete - Missing context
```

---

### Round 3: Context & Depth (≤5 questions)

**Purpose:** Understand broader context and competitive landscape

**Questions:**

1. **Campaign Context**
   - "Is this content standalone or part of a larger campaign?"
   - If campaign: "What's the campaign theme/message?"
   - If standalone: "What's the occasion or trigger for this content?"

2. **Competitive Differentiation**
   - "What makes your offering different from competitors?"
   - Follow-up: "What's your unique value proposition?"
   - Alternative: "Why should someone choose you over alternatives?"

3. **Audience Pain Points**
   - "What are the main problems or frustrations your audience faces?"
   - Follow-up: "How does your product/service solve these?"
   - Alternative: "What transformation do you provide?"

4. **Seasonal/Cultural Context**
   - "Are there any seasonal or cultural considerations?"
   - Examples: Ramadan, Eid, holidays, events, trends
   - Follow-up: "Any timing sensitivities?"

5. **Previous Performance**
   - "What has worked well in your previous content?"
   - Follow-up: "What hasn't worked?"
   - Alternative: "Any specific engagement patterns you've noticed?"

**After Round 3:**

```yaml
Summary:
  Campaign: [standalone/campaign theme]
  Differentiation: [unique value]
  Pain Points: [list]
  Cultural Context: [considerations]
  Previous Insights: [what worked/didn't work]

Complete Brief:
  ✓ Brand & Industry
  ✓ Goal & Action
  ✓ Audience
  ✓ Platform & Format
  ✓ Voice & Tone
  ✓ Constraints
  ✓ Context

Final CQL:
  "What [format] will drive [specific audience] to [specific action] 
   on [platform] while maintaining [brand voice] and [key constraint]?"
  
Status: ✅ Complete - Ready for CQL Lock
```

## Adaptive Questioning

### If User Provides Partial Information

**Scenario:** User says "I need an Instagram post for my skincare brand"

**Response:**

```
Great! I can help with that. Let me gather a few key details:

1. What's the primary goal? (awareness/engagement/sales)
2. Who's your target audience? (age, location, skin concerns)
3. What action should they take? (like, comment, click link, buy)
4. What's your brand voice? (professional/casual/scientific)
5. Any specific message or product to highlight?
```

### If User Provides Complete Brief

**Scenario:** User provides detailed brief upfront

**Response:**

```
Excellent! You've provided a comprehensive brief:
✓ Brand: [name]
✓ Goal: [goal]
✓ Audience: [audience]
✓ Platform: [platform]
✓ Voice: [voice]

Let me confirm the Content Question Lock:
"What [format] will drive [audience] to [action] on [platform] 
 while maintaining [voice]?"

Is this correct? If yes, I'll proceed to content creation.
```

### If User Is Vague

**Scenario:** User says "Make it engaging"

**Response:**

```
I want to ensure I create the most effective content for you. 
"Engaging" can mean different things for different audiences. 

Could you help me understand:
1. Engaging for whom? (who's your target audience?)
2. Engaging how? (likes, comments, shares, clicks?)
3. On which platform?

This will help me create content that truly resonates.
```

## Question Bank by Category

### Brand Understanding

- "What is your brand's mission or purpose?"
- "What are your brand values?"
- "How long have you been in business?"
- "What's your brand personality?"

### Audience Deep Dive

- "What keeps your audience up at night?"
- "What are their aspirations?"
- "Where do they hang out online?"
- "What language/slang do they use?"
- "What influences their decisions?"

### Content Context

- "What's happening in your industry right now?"
- "Any recent news or trends to leverage?"
- "What are competitors talking about?"
- "Any upcoming events or launches?"

### Performance Insights

- "What's your best-performing content?"
- "What got the most engagement?"
- "What drove the most conversions?"
- "Any content that flopped? Why?"

### Constraints & Preferences

- "Any brand guidelines to follow?"
- "Preferred hashtag strategy?"
- "Emoji usage preferences?"
- "Visual style preferences?"

## Best Practices

### DO

✅ Ask open-ended questions when possible  
✅ Listen for unstated needs  
✅ Summarize to confirm understanding  
✅ Adapt questions based on responses  
✅ Respect the 5-question limit  

### DON'T

❌ Ask questions already answered  
❌ Overwhelm with too many questions  
❌ Make assumptions without confirming  
❌ Skip to content creation prematurely  
❌ Ignore red flags or contradictions

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Rushed Client

**Client:** "I need this ASAP, just write something!"

**Response:**

```
I understand the urgency! To create effective content quickly, 
I need just 3 critical pieces of information:

1. Who's the audience?
2. What action should they take?
3. What's your brand voice?

This will take 2 minutes and ensure the content hits the mark.
```

### Scenario 2: Uncertain Client

**Client:** "I'm not sure what I want..."

**Response:**

```
No problem! Let's start simple:

1. What's the business goal right now? (more awareness/sales/engagement)
2. Who are you trying to reach?
3. What platform are you most active on?

We'll build from there.
```

### Scenario 3: Over-Detailed Client

**Client:** [Provides 20 paragraphs of information]

**Response:**

```
Thank you for the detailed information! Let me summarize the key points:

[Concise summary]

Is this accurate? Any critical details I missed?
```
