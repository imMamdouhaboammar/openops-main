## CRO Frameworks (2025)

### 1. The Lever Framework ✅ NEW

- **Source**: 2025 CRO research
- **5 Levers**: Usability, Motivation, Comprehension, Trust, Cost
- **Application**: Diagnose bottlenecks in conversion funnel

### 2. RICE Prioritization

- **Formula**: (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort
- **Use**: Experiment prioritization
- **Threshold**: RICE > 10,000 = High Priority

### 3. PIE Framework

- **Formula**: (Potential + Importance + Ease) / 3
- **Use**: Page prioritization for testing
- **Scale**: 1-10 for each factor

## Persuasion Psychology

### Cialdini's 7 Principles

1. **Reciprocity**: Free trials, lead magnets
2. **Commitment & Consistency**: Multi-step forms, progress bars
3. **Social Proof**: Testimonials, "X customers served"
4. **Authority**: Expert endorsements, certifications
5. **Liking**: Relatable brand story, values alignment
6. **Scarcity & Urgency**: "Only 3 left", countdown timers
7. **Unity**: Community language, shared identity

### Cognitive Biases for CRO

- **Anchoring**: Show original price vs. discounted
- **Loss Aversion**: "Don't miss out on $500 savings"
- **Cognitive Load**: Simplify choices (3 pricing tiers, not 7)
- **Paradox of Choice**: Too many options = paralysis

## Case Studies

### 1. Dropbox (Referral Program)

- **Tactic**: Give 500MB storage for each referred friend
- **Result**: 4M users in 15 months (60% from referrals)
- **Principle Applied**: Reciprocity, viral loop

### 2. Booking.com (Social Proof)

- **Tactic**: "X people booked this hotel in last 24 hours"
- **Result**: 12% conversion rate lift
- **Principle Applied**: Social proof, urgency

### 3. Obama Campaign (Multi-Step Form)

- **Tactic**: Ask email first, then more details (commitment & consistency)
- **Result**: $60M additional donations
- **Principle Applied**: Micro-commitments
