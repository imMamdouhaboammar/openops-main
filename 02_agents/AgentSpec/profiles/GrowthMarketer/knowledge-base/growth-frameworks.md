## 1. Growth Marketing Frameworks

### The Hook Model (Nir Eyal)

Build habit-forming products with a 4-step loop:

1. **Trigger**: External (email, push) or Internal (emotion, routine)
2. **Action**: Simplest behavior in anticipation of reward
3. **Variable Reward**: Unpredictable gratification (social, hunt, self)
4. **Investment**: User puts something in = stored value, loads next trigger

**Example: Instagram**

- Trigger: Push notification "John liked your photo"
- Action: Open app
- Variable Reward: See how many likes, comments (social validation)
- Investment: Post new photo → stored in profile, increases future engagement

### Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

Customers "hire" products to get a job done. Understand the progress they're trying to make.

**Framework**:

- **Functional Job**: What task needs to be accomplished?
- **Emotional Job**: How do they want to feel?
- **Social Job**: How do they want to be perceived?

**Example: Milkshake Study**

- Functional: Fill stomach during commute
- Emotional: Combat boredom
- Social: Something to do while driving

### Lean Analytics (Alistair Croll)

Focus on the ONE metric that matters for your stage:

| Stage | Focus Metric |
|-------|--------------|
| Empathy | Problem/Solution fit discovery |
| Stickiness | Engagement, retention |
| Virality | Viral coefficient > 1 |
| Revenue | CLV, ARPU |
| Scale | Market share, category dominance |

## 2. Growth Hacking Tactics

### Dropbox Referral Program

**Tactic**: Double-sided incentive

- Referrer gets: +500MB storage
- Referred gets: +500MB storage
**Result**: 3900% growth in 15 months

### Airbnb's Craigslist Integration

**Tactic**: Cross-post Airbnb listings to Craigslist

- Massive distribution via existing platform
- Reverse-engineered Craigslist API
**Result**: Early growth before Craigslist shut it down

### Hotmail "PS: I Love You"

**Tactic**: Email signature in every email sent

- "PS: I Love You. Get your free email at Hotmail"
**Result**: 12 million users in 18 months (1996-1997)

### LinkedIn's "Congratulate" Feature

**Tactic**: Prompt users to congratulate connections on job changes

- Increases engagement
- Re-activates dormant users
- Creates viral loop (notifications)

## 3. Experimentation Playbook

### Hypothesis Template

```
WHO: [User segment]
WHAT: [Change we're making]
WHY: [Reason/insight]
EXPECTED OUTCOME: [Metric] will improve by [X%]

Example:
WHO: Free trial users in their first 7 days
WHAT: Send personalized email with use case matching their industry
WHY: Generic emails have low engagement; personalization builds relevance
EXPECTED OUTCOME: Day 7 activation rate will improve from 42% to 55%
```

### Prioritization Framework: PIE

- **Potential**: How much improvement can you expect?
- **Importance**: How valuable is traffic to this page?
- **Ease**: How difficult is implementation?

Score each 1-10, calculate (P + I + E) / 3

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Peeking**: Checking results before test completion → false positives
2. **Sample Pollution**: Changing test mid-flight
3. **Ignoring Segments**: Aggregate win might hide segment losses
4. **Novelty Effect**: Users engage with new things temporarily
5. **Seasonality**: Run minimum 1-2 weeks to account for day-of-week patterns

## 4. Retention Tactics Library

### Win-Back Campaigns

**Trigger**: User inactive for 30/60/90 days

**Email Sequence**:

1. Day 30: "We miss you! Here's what you've been missing"
2. Day 45: "Special offer: 20% off to come back"
3. Day 60: "Last chance before we archive your account"

### Engagement Loops

- **Streaks**: Duolingo's daily streak (don't break the chain!)
- **Leaderboards**: Strava's segment rankings
- **Challenges**: Fitbit's step challenges
- **Progress Bars**: LinkedIn profile completeness

### Churn Signals

- Login frequency ↓
- Feature usage ↓
- Support tickets ↑
- NPS score < 7
- Billing issue

## 5. Viral Mechanics

### Network Effects Types

1. **Direct**: More users = more value (WhatsApp, Facebook)
2. **Two-Sided**: Buyers attract sellers, sellers attract buyers (eBay, Uber)
3. **Data**: More usage = better product (Waze, Google Maps)

### Shareability Criteria

- **Incentive**: Reward for sharing
- **Ease**: One-click sharing
- **Relevance**: Shareable content is valuable to recipient
- **Social Currency**: Sharing makes me look good

### Viral Loop Examples

**Zoom**:

- Every meeting link is a Zoom ad
- Non-users can join without account → Low friction
- Host looks professional using Zoom → Social currency

**Calendly**:

- Every booking link promotes Calendly
- Recipient experiences ease of scheduling
- They sign up for own account
