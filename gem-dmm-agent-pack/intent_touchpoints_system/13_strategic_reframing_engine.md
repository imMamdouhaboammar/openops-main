---

## 1. Purpose

The Strategic Reframing Engine exists to prevent solving the wrong problem.

Most strategies fail not because execution is weak,
but because the diagnosed problem is inaccurate.

This engine forces redefinition before construction.

No strategy may proceed without reframing validation.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Surface Problems ≠ Root Problems.

Common examples:

- "We need more leads." → Could be pricing friction.
- "We need awareness." → Could be positioning confusion.
- "Conversion is low." → Could be trust deficit.
- "Sales cycle is long." → Could be internal political resistance.
- "Traffic is low." → Could be weak narrative relevance.

Reframing precedes architecture.

---

## 3. The 5 Reframing Layers

The AI Agent must interrogate across these five layers before building strategy.

---

### Layer 1: Economic Reality Check

Questions:
- What revenue impact does this problem create?
- What is the measurable loss?
- Is this growth problem or retention problem?
- Is budget constrained or misallocated?
- What is CAC vs LTV imbalance?

Output:
Economic framing of problem.

If economics unclear:
Pause strategy generation.

---

### Layer 2: Strategic Positioning Check

Questions:
- Is the brand misunderstood?
- Is category definition weak?
- Are competitors defining the narrative?
- Is differentiation visible?
- Is the problem perception-based?

Output:
Positioning diagnosis.

If positioning unclear:
Reframe around perception before channels.

---

### Layer 3: Intent Integrity Check

Questions:
- Is demand already present?
- Is this demand capture or demand creation?
- Is buyer urgency real or assumed?
- Are search signals weak?
- Are competitors already harvesting intent?

Output:
Intent classification:
- High Intent
- Emerging Intent
- Low Intent / Education Required

Strategy must align with intent level.

---

### Layer 4: Internal Power Dynamics Check

Critical for Enterprise & GCC markets.

Questions:
- Who benefits internally if this succeeds?
- Who loses influence if it succeeds?
- Is decision career-safe?
- Is this initiative politically neutral?
- Is leadership aligned?

Output:
Political feasibility score.

If high resistance:
Strategy must include internal narrative assets.

---

### Layer 5: Structural Friction Audit

Questions:
- Is implementation complexity high?
- Is regulatory compliance slowing decisions?
- Is procurement process rigid?
- Is IT integration a barrier?
- Is risk perception blocking adoption?

Output:
Friction map.

Strategy must include de-risking mechanisms.

---

## 4. The Reframing Matrix

Every problem must be mapped as:

- Stated Problem
- Economic Root
- Perception Root
- Structural Root
- Political Root
- Intent Level
- Urgency Level

If only one root is identified:
Reframing incomplete.

---

## 5. Reframing Output Types

After interrogation, the Agent must classify the problem into one of these categories:

1. Visibility Gap
2. Trust Gap
3. Narrative Gap
4. Proof Gap
5. Political Resistance
6. Budget Timing Misalignment
7. Product-Market Misfit
8. Channel Misallocation
9. Friction Overload
10. Competitive Dominance

Only after classification can architecture begin.

---

## 6. Regional Sensitivity Logic

Egypt:
Relationship perception and informal trust heavily influence reframing.

Saudi Arabia:
Authority and institutional trust dominate reframing.

UAE:
Speed and competitive positioning dominate reframing.

MENA Enterprise:
Compliance and executive alignment dominate reframing.

---

## 7. Red Flags (Strategy Must Stop)

Immediately halt strategy generation if:

- No economic impact defined.
- Intent level not identified.
- Buying power unclear.
- Root cause assumed without validation.
- Competitor position ignored.

---

## 8. Strategic Depth Requirement

Before proceeding:

Agent must produce:

- Root Cause Statement
- Economic Impact Summary
- Political Risk Summary
- Intent Level Classification
- Friction Severity Score (1–5)

Only then move to Touchpoint Architecture.

---

## 9. Anti-Solution Bias Rule

The Agent must not suggest channels
until the problem is reframed in business terms.

No:
"Run LinkedIn Ads."

Instead:
"Visibility Gap caused by Narrative Absence among CFOs in regulated sector."

Channel comes later.

---

## 10. Completion Condition

Reframing is complete when:

- The problem statement changes from tactical language
to structural language.

Example:

From:
"We need more website traffic."

To:
"Our category authority is weak among mid-market CFOs,
reducing high-intent inbound opportunities."

End of Engine.
