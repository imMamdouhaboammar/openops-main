# Competitive Intelligence Framework
Version: 1.0
Region Scope: Egypt – GCC – MENA
Applies To: B2B, B2C, D2C, Enterprise, Government, Regulated Markets

---

## 1. Purpose

Competitive analysis is not about listing competitors.

It is about:
- Identifying who threatens your intent capture.
- Understanding where power is concentrated.
- Prioritizing who deserves strategic attention.

This framework prevents strategic overreaction.

---

## 2. Step One: Define What "Competition" Means

A competitor is not:
- Anyone selling something similar.

A competitor is:
- Any entity that intercepts the same buying intent you want to capture.

Competition is intent-based, not category-based.

---

## 3. Competitor Identification Layers

The Agent must classify competitors into 5 types:

---

### Layer 1: Direct Competitors

Definition:
Offer similar solution to same target segment.

Example:
Two B2B insurance providers targeting regulated enterprises in KSA.

Threat Level:
High.

---

### Layer 2: Indirect Competitors

Definition:
Offer alternative solution to same problem.

Example:
Cyber insurance vs internal risk mitigation consultancy.

Threat Level:
Medium to High.

---

### Layer 3: Budget Competitors

Definition:
Compete for the same budget, not same product.

Example:
Insurance provider vs enterprise compliance SaaS.

Both fight for risk management budget.

Threat Level:
Contextual.

---

### Layer 4: Status Quo Competitor

Definition:
The decision to do nothing.

In MENA markets, inertia is powerful.

Threat Level:
Extremely High.

---

### Layer 5: Emerging / Latent Competitors

Definition:
Not competing now, but positioned to enter.

Example:
Global brand expanding into GCC.
Tech platform planning regional launch.

Threat Level:
Future structural risk.

---

## 4. Competitor Filtering Matrix

Not all competitors deserve focus.

The Agent must score each competitor across:

| Criteria | Score 1-5 |
|----------|------------|
| Market Overlap |
| Authority Strength |
| Distribution Strength |
| Digital Visibility |
| Pricing Power |
| Regional Alignment |
| Proof Density |
| Narrative Control |

Output:
Competitor Priority Index (CPI)

Focus only on Top 3 CPI.

Never design strategy around more than 3 primary competitors.

---

## 5. Competitive Archetype Classification

The Agent must assign each competitor to one archetype:

1. The Authority Giant
2. The Aggressive Discounter
3. The Digital Native
4. The Relationship Insider
5. The Regulatory Insider
6. The Legacy Brand
7. The Specialist Boutique

Strategy depends on archetype.

Never fight archetypes the same way.

---

## 6. Signal-Based Competitive Analysis

Instead of reading websites, analyze signals:

- Hiring patterns
- Partnership announcements
- Conference participation
- Thought leadership topics
- Ad visibility
- Website SEO footprint
- Case study volume
- Executive LinkedIn activity

Signals reveal momentum.

Momentum matters more than messaging.

---

## 7. Intent Interception Mapping

For each competitor, map:

- Which stage of the journey they dominate.
- Where they intercept buyers.
- Where they are weak.

Output:

| Competitor | Dominant Stage | Weak Stage | Interception Point |

Example:
Competitor A dominates Education via SEO.
Weak in Human Touchpoints.
Opportunity: Executive-level engagement.

---

## 8. Resource Efficiency Rule

Strategy must avoid:

- Fighting dominant competitor on their strongest terrain.
- Matching ad spend blindly.
- Copying messaging tone.
- Engaging in price war.

Instead:
Exploit asymmetry.

---

## 9. Competitive Red Flags

The Agent must avoid:

- Overestimating large global brands in local MENA context.
- Underestimating relationship-based competitors in GCC.
- Ignoring government-backed players.
- Ignoring informal networks.

Competition in MENA is hybrid:
Digital + Relationship + Regulatory.

---

## 10. Strategic Output Requirement

After analysis, the Agent must produce:

1. Top 3 Primary Competitors
2. Their Archetypes
3. Their Stronghold Territory
4. Their Structural Weakness
5. Strategic Entry Angle
6. Resource Allocation Priority

If strategy references more than 3 main competitors,
it is diluted.

---

## 11. Completion Condition

Competitive intelligence phase is complete when:

- The battlefield is clear.
- Focus is narrowed.
- Overreaction is eliminated.
- Strategic direction is defined by asymmetry.

If everything looks like competition,
analysis is immature.

End of Framework.
