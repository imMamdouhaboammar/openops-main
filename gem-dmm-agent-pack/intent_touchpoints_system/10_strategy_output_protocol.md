# Strategy Output Protocol
Version: 1.0  
Region Focus: Egypt – GCC – MENA  
Applies To: B2B / B2C / D2C / Services / Retail / F&B / SaaS / Insurance / Healthcare / Fintech  

---

## Purpose

This protocol defines how the AI Agent converts:

- Intent Mapping
- Touchpoint Architecture
- Bridge Logic
- Buying Committee Intelligence
- Measurement Signals
- Experimentation Engine

into a **clear, executable, client-ready strategy document.**

The output must be structured, logical, and operational — not inspirational.

---

# Core Output Philosophy

The strategy must answer ONE central question:

> “How do we systematically increase certainty before the purchase decision?”

NOT:
- “How do we get traffic?”
- “How do we post more content?”
- “How do we run ads?”

---

# Strategy Output Structure

The AI Agent must generate the strategy in this exact sequence:

---

## 1. Executive Context

- Market reality (Egypt / GCC / MENA specific)
- Competitive landscape snapshot
- Decision complexity level
- Deal cycle length
- Risk sensitivity level

Output: Clear environment framing.

---

## 2. Intent Landscape Overview

Identify:

- High-intent segments
- Mid-intent segments
- Latent demand segments
- Buying triggers
- Friction points

Output:
Intent Map Table:
Segment | Intent Stage | Primary Question | Risk Level | Trigger Type

---

## 3. Psychological Decision Map

Define:

- What the buyer fears
- What the buyer must believe
- What the buyer must prove internally
- Who influences the decision

Output:
Decision Friction Matrix.

---

## 4. Touchpoint Architecture Plan

For each intent stage:

Awareness  
Education  
Validation  
Human  
Conversion  

Define:

- Required touchpoints
- Required proof elements
- Required bridge assets
- Channel layer (LinkedIn, Events, Search, PR, Direct)

Output:
Touchpoint Blueprint Table.

---

## 5. Buying Committee Penetration Plan (If B2B)

Define:

- Economic authority path
- Technical authority path
- Operational path
- Informal influence path

Output:
Committee Engagement Map.

If B2C/D2C:
Replace with Emotional Decision Path Mapping.

---

## 6. Bridge Content Deployment Plan

For each transition:

Education → Validation  
Validation → Human  
Human → Conversion  

Define:

- Bridge asset type
- Format
- Core psychological job
- Risk reduced
- Open loop created

Output:
Bridge Deployment Table.

---

## 7. Orchestration Layer

Define:

- When Marketing acts
- When Sales enters
- When Partners activate
- When PR amplifies

Output:
Timeline View (8–12 week minimum).

---

## 8. Measurement & Signal Layer

Define:

- Intent velocity metrics
- Committee coverage metrics
- Stage transition rates
- Risk reduction indicators
- Deal velocity shifts

Output:
Account-Level KPI Scorecard.

---

## 9. Experimentation Roadmap

Identify:

- 3 immediate experiments
- 3 medium-cycle experiments
- 3 structural experiments

Each experiment must define:

- Hypothesis
- Stage targeted
- Success metric
- Time to learn

---

## 10. 90-Day Execution Snapshot

Must include:

Weeks 1–2 → Foundation  
Weeks 3–6 → Activation  
Weeks 7–10 → Penetration  
Weeks 11–13 → Optimization  

Each week must specify:

- Objective
- Assets required
- Owner
- Measurement signal

---

# Output Format Rules

The strategy must:

- Avoid generic advice.
- Avoid listing services.
- Avoid channel-first thinking.
- Be stage-driven.
- Be friction-driven.
- Be certainty-driven.

---

# Regional Adaptation Rules

Egypt:
- Trust is built through personal credibility + social validation.
- Over-formal messaging reduces conversion.

Saudi Arabia:
- Authority proof and compliance are mandatory.
- Executive-level presence increases decision comfort.

UAE:
- Speed and clarity matter more than narrative depth.
- Competitive framing is stronger lever.

---

# Disallowed Strategy Patterns

The AI Agent must reject strategies that:

- Start with "We will run ads."
- Focus on impressions or engagement only.
- Ignore buying committee complexity.
- Skip bridge logic.
- Present disconnected touchpoints.
- Measure vanity metrics.

---

# Final Output Standard

A strategy is complete only if:

- Every touchpoint has a psychological role.
- Every stage has proof.
- Every transition has a bridge.
- Every committee role is mapped.
- Every KPI measures progression, not activity.

---

# Ultimate Rule

The strategy must feel inevitable.

When the client reads it, the reaction should be:

"This is exactly how our buyers think."

Not:

"This is another marketing proposal."
