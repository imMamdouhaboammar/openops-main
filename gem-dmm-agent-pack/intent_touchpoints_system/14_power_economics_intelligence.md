## 1. Purpose

Most strategic failures happen not because value is weak,
but because power dynamics were ignored.

This engine forces the AI Agent to map:

- Who controls budget
- Who controls narrative
- Who controls risk approval
- Who influences the informal decision

Before designing any Touchpoint Architecture.

No strategy may proceed without power mapping.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Value does not close deals.
Power alignment closes deals.

In MENA markets especially:

- Formal authority ≠ Real influence
- Budget holder ≠ Decision maker
- End user ≠ Final approver
- Procurement ≠ Strategic sponsor

Understanding power structure precedes messaging design.

---

## 3. The 4 Power Layers

The Agent must map all four layers.

---

### Layer 1: Budget Power

Questions:
- Who owns the budget?
- Is budget pre-approved or request-based?
- Is this OPEX or CAPEX?
- Is spending centralized or department-controlled?
- Is budget cycle annual, quarterly, or project-based?

Output:
Budget control clarity.

If unclear:
Strategy must include budget education assets.

---

### Layer 2: Political Power

Questions:
- Who gains visibility if this succeeds?
- Who risks reputation if it fails?
- Is this initiative career-safe?
- Is leadership pushing or resisting change?
- Is there a dominant internal personality shaping decisions?

Output:
Political risk score (Low / Medium / High).

If high:
Strategy must include internal justification tools.

---

### Layer 3: Informal Influence

Critical in Egypt and GCC.

Questions:
- Who has personal trust influence?
- Is there a board member involved?
- Are brokers or consultants shaping evaluation?
- Are external advisors influencing perception?
- Is “wasta” or relationship credibility relevant?

Output:
Influence map.

If strong informal layer:
Strategy must include ecosystem engagement.

---

### Layer 4: Economic Urgency

Questions:
- Is this problem financially urgent?
- Is it compliance-driven?
- Is it competitive pressure?
- Is it efficiency-driven?
- Is there regulatory deadline pressure?

Output:
Urgency Index (1–5).

Low urgency = Awareness strategy.
High urgency = Capture strategy.

---

## 4. Economic Sensitivity Mapping

The Agent must classify the economic sensitivity type:

1. Revenue Expansion
2. Cost Reduction
3. Risk Mitigation
4. Compliance Necessity
5. Reputation Protection
6. Operational Efficiency
7. Market Entry Enablement

Each sensitivity requires different touchpoints.

Example:
Risk Mitigation requires Proof & Authority.
Revenue Expansion requires ROI Narratives.

---

## 5. Regional Behavioral Patterns

Egypt:
- Decision influenced by relationship trust.
- Budget flexibility varies.
- Informal validation often critical.

Saudi Arabia:
- Authority-driven structure.
- Compliance and hierarchy matter.
- Public reputation significant.

UAE:
- Speed and competitiveness matter.
- ROI clarity expected.
- Procurement structured.

MENA Enterprise:
- Multi-layer evaluation.
- Consultant-heavy decisions.
- Strong risk aversion.

Strategy must adapt accordingly.

---

## 6. Power Risk Red Flags

Strategy must pause if:

- Budget owner unknown.
- Decision authority unclear.
- Political resistance high.
- Informal influencers ignored.
- Urgency low but aggressive conversion planned.

---

## 7. Power & Economics Output Requirement

Before Touchpoint design, the Agent must produce:

- Budget Control Map
- Influence Map
- Political Risk Score
- Urgency Index
- Economic Sensitivity Classification

Without this layer, strategy is incomplete.

---

## 8. Alignment Rule

Touchpoints must align with power reality.

Examples:

If CFO dominant:
→ Financial proof content required.

If CEO brand-driven:
→ Executive visibility touchpoints required.

If Procurement dominant:
→ Process clarity assets required.

If Consultant gatekeeping:
→ Industry authority content required.

---

## 9. Completion Condition

Power Intelligence is complete when:

The strategy shifts from:

"We need awareness."

To:

"The CFO controls budget approval, Procurement enforces structure,
and the CEO is risk-averse.
Touchpoints must reduce perceived risk and justify ROI in financial terms."

Only after this may architecture begin.
