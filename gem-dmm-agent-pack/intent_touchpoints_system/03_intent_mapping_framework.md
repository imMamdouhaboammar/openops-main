# Intent Mapping Framework
Version: 1.0
Region Focus: Egypt – GCC – MENA
Foundation: Intent-First Strategic Thinking

---

## 1. Purpose

This framework exists to force strategic clarity before execution.

No touchpoints.
No channels.
No campaigns.

Until the real intent is mapped.

Intent Mapping answers one critical question:

"What is truly happening inside the buyer's mind?"

---

## 2. Intent Is Not What The Client Says

Clients often describe symptoms.

Examples:

- "We need more leads."
- "We want brand awareness."
- "Sales are slow."
- "We need social media content."

These are surface-level requests.

Intent Mapping identifies:

- The business pressure.
- The internal fear.
- The measurable gap.
- The strategic tension.

---

## 3. The 4 Layers of Intent

The AI Agent must diagnose all four layers.

### Layer 1: Stated Intent (Surface)

What the client explicitly says.

Example:
"We want more enterprise clients."

---

### Layer 2: Functional Intent (Operational)

What operational gap actually exists.

Examples:
- Weak positioning.
- Long sales cycle.
- Low trust signals.
- Poor qualification.
- Undefined ICP.
- No authority presence.

---

### Layer 3: Emotional Intent (Psychological)

What decision-makers are worried about.

In MENA markets this often includes:

- Reputation risk.
- Executive pressure.
- Competitive comparison.
- Budget justification.
- Fear of failure.

This layer drives behavior more than logic.

---

### Layer 4: Strategic Intent (Outcome-Level)

What the organization ultimately wants.

Examples:

- Market leadership.
- Category authority.
- Expansion into GCC.
- Higher deal size.
- Shorter decision cycle.
- Institutional credibility.

This is the north star.

---

## 4. The Intent Diagnosis Process

The AI Agent must execute this sequence:

Step 1:
Restate the surface request.

Step 2:
Identify measurable performance gaps.

Step 3:
Analyze competitor movement.

Step 4:
Identify internal friction points.

Step 5:
Define the true strategic intent in one sentence.

Format:

"The real intent is not X.
The real intent is Y."

---

## 5. Intent Categories

The AI Agent must classify intent into one of the following:

1. Growth Intent
2. Trust Intent
3. Differentiation Intent
4. Market Entry Intent
5. Authority Intent
6. Conversion Efficiency Intent
7. Reputation Repair Intent
8. Competitive Defense Intent

Every strategy must align with exactly one primary intent.

Multiple intents create chaos.

---

## 6. Intent Intensity Levels

Not all intent is equal.

The AI Agent must score intensity:

Low Intent:
Exploration stage. No urgency.

Moderate Intent:
Problem awareness. Early evaluation.

High Intent:
Active comparison. Budget allocated.

Critical Intent:
Immediate decision pressure.

Touchpoint sequencing changes based on intensity.

---

## 7. Regional Behavioral Modifiers

Egypt:
- Relationship-first buying.
- Informal trust building.
- Strong influence of referrals.

GCC:
- Institutional reputation heavy.
- Executive alignment critical.
- Formal documentation expected.
- Risk avoidance high.

MENA Enterprise:
- Committee-driven decisions.
- Cross-border trust sensitivity.
- Authority signals mandatory.

Intent Mapping must incorporate these modifiers.

---

## 8. Output Format Requirement

After mapping, the AI Agent must produce:

1. Primary Strategic Intent
2. Intent Intensity Level
3. Decision-Maker Emotional Drivers
4. Core Friction Points
5. Strategic Tension Statement
6. Intent Summary (one paragraph maximum)

No strategy can proceed without this output.

---

## 9. Strategic Guardrail

If intent is unclear:

Do not build touchpoints.
Do not recommend channels.
Do not propose campaigns.

Ask for clarification.

Intent clarity is mandatory.

---

## 10. Final Principle

Strategy built without intent clarity results in:

- Fragmented messaging
- Wasted budget
- Misaligned touchpoints
- Weak positioning
- Internal confusion

Intent Mapping is the foundation layer.

No shortcuts.

End of Framework.
