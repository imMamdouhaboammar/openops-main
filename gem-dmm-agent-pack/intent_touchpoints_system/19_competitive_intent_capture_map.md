# Competitive Intent Capture Map
Version: 1.0
Region Scope: Egypt – GCC – MENA
Applies To: B2B, B2C, D2C, Enterprise, Regulated Markets

---

## 1. Purpose

This framework identifies:

- Who captures buying intent before you.
- At which stage competitors dominate perception.
- Where decision momentum is shaped.
- Where strategic interception is possible.

This is not market share analysis.
This is intent share analysis.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Revenue follows intent.
Intent follows perception.
Perception follows visibility + proof + narrative repetition.

Whoever controls early-stage perception
controls late-stage decision probability.

---

## 3. Intent Stage Mapping

The Agent must map competitors across 5 intent stages:

1. Latent Intent (Problem Awareness)
2. Active Research (Solution Exploration)
3. Vendor Comparison (Shortlisting)
4. Internal Justification (Committee Review)
5. Decision Confidence (Final Risk Check)

---

## 4. Intent Capture Grid

For each primary competitor, the Agent must complete:

| Competitor | Dominant Stage | Narrative Position | Authority Level | Proof Density | Weak Stage | Interception Opportunity |

Example:

Competitor A:
- Dominant Stage: Active Research
- Narrative Position: "Market Leader"
- Authority Level: High (Strong SEO + Events)
- Proof Density: Moderate
- Weak Stage: Internal Justification
- Opportunity: Financial ROI modeling + Risk documentation

---

## 5. Narrative Ownership Analysis

The Agent must answer:

- Who defines the problem language?
- Who ranks first for problem-related search?
- Who appears in AI answers and summaries?
- Who is quoted in media?
- Who educates the market?

If competitor language becomes market language,
they own the frame.

Frame ownership > price competition.

---

## 6. Buying Committee Intent Capture

Different competitors may dominate different roles:

Map influence per role:

| Role | Competitor Influence Level | Dominant Messaging | Vulnerability |

Roles:
- Economic Decision Maker
- Technical Decision Maker
- Executive Sponsor
- Risk/Compliance
- Procurement
- End User

Example:
Competitor B dominates Technical Buyer through documentation depth.
Weak with CFO due to poor financial modeling.

Opportunity:
Build Financial Risk Narrative.

---

## 7. Search & Digital Intent Mapping

The Agent must evaluate:

- Who ranks for high-intent keywords?
- Who owns featured snippets?
- Who appears in AI Overviews?
- Who dominates LinkedIn thought leadership?
- Who runs paid campaigns on core terms?

Measure:
Visibility Share vs Intent Quality.

High traffic does not equal high intent control.

---

## 8. Proof Density Mapping

Proof is competitive weapon.

For each competitor:

Count:
- Case studies
- Local references
- Industry-specific references
- Regulatory approvals
- Enterprise logos
- Testimonials

Proof Density Score:
Low / Medium / High

If competitor has high proof density,
price competition is ineffective.

---

## 9. Risk Perception Analysis (Critical in GCC)

In MENA markets,
decision-making is risk-averse.

Map:
- Who appears safer?
- Who appears established?
- Who appears politically aligned?
- Who appears compliant?

Safety perception often overrides innovation.

---

## 10. Intent Leakage Identification

Intent leakage occurs when:

- Competitor captures early education stage.
- You only appear at conversion stage.
- Buyer arrives already biased.

The Agent must detect:

- At which stage you are invisible.
- Where you enter too late.
- Where perception gap exists.

---

## 11. Strategic Output Requirements

After mapping, the Agent must produce:

1. Intent Capture Dominance Map (visual or structured summary)
2. Primary Competitor Per Stage
3. Frame Ownership Analysis
4. Committee Influence Distribution
5. Proof Density Ranking
6. Risk Perception Comparison
7. 3 Strategic Interception Moves

Example Interception Moves:
- Own financial modeling narrative.
- Build regional proof campaign.
- Target executive sponsor via PR positioning.

---

## 12. Strategic Rule

Do not attack competitors where they dominate.

Instead:
- Enter at weak stage.
- Reframe narrative.
- Build proof where they lack.
- Control committee angle they ignore.

---

## 13. Completion Condition

The map is complete when:

- You know where intent starts.
- You know who shapes it.
- You know where it shifts.
- You know where you can intercept without brute force.

If strategy does not specify interception points,
it is reactive.

End of Framework.
