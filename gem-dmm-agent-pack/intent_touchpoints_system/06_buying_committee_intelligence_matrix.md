## 1. Purpose

This file defines how strategy adapts to multiple decision actors.

In high-ticket environments across MENA,
you do not sell to a company.

You navigate a committee.

Each role evaluates risk differently.
Each role requires different proof.
Each role enters the journey at a different stage.

This matrix ensures touchpoints are distributed correctly.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Authority ≠ Influence.

Some stakeholders sign.
Others block.
Others accelerate.
Others delay.

Strategy must map:

- Decision Power
- Influence Power
- Risk Sensitivity
- Information Depth Required
- Preferred Touchpoint Type

---

## 3. Core Roles (Expanded Model)

This model is adaptable to B2B, B2C, D2C (complex purchases), Government, Regulated sectors, and Enterprise.

---

### 1. Economic Authority

Typical Titles:
- CFO
- Finance Director
- Group Finance Head
- Founder (SMEs)
- Managing Partner

Primary Concern:
Financial exposure and risk control.

Core Questions:
- What is ROI?
- What is payback period?
- What happens if this fails?
- Is this defensible internally?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Validation (case studies with numbers)
- Risk analysis
- Cost comparison models
- Scenario simulations

Bridge Requirements:
Validation → Conversion bridges must include process clarity and financial transparency.

Regional Note:
In GCC, financial governance scrutiny is high in large entities.
In Egypt, liquidity impact concerns are stronger in mid-market.

---

### 2. Strategic Authority

Typical Titles:
- CEO
- Managing Director
- Country Head
- General Manager

Primary Concern:
Strategic positioning and long-term impact.

Core Questions:
- Does this align with our growth vision?
- Is this future-proof?
- How does this affect reputation?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Presence (PR, media, events)
- Executive thought leadership
- Market positioning content

Bridge Requirements:
Presence → Education bridges must focus on industry trends.

---

### 3. Technical Authority

Typical Titles:
- CTO
- IT Director
- Operations Manager
- Systems Architect
- Marketing Technology Lead

Primary Concern:
Implementation feasibility and operational stability.

Core Questions:
- Will this integrate?
- What are the technical risks?
- What is the migration cost?
- Is the system secure?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Deep documentation
- Technical webinars
- Security certifications
- Process diagrams

Bridge Requirements:
Education → Validation bridges must include real deployment scenarios.

Regional Note:
GCC enterprises heavily evaluate compliance and integration risk.
Egypt mid-market focuses more on resource limitations.

---

### 4. Operational Owner

Typical Titles:
- Department Manager
- Operations Lead
- HR Manager
- Marketing Manager

Primary Concern:
Daily usability and workload impact.

Core Questions:
- Will this make my life easier?
- Is training required?
- Will my KPIs improve?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Practical demos
- Use-case walkthroughs
- Process simplification examples

Bridge Requirements:
Human → Conversion bridges must reduce fear of operational complexity.

---

### 5. Risk & Compliance Gatekeeper

Typical Titles:
- Legal Counsel
- Compliance Officer
- Risk Manager
- Governance Head

Primary Concern:
Regulatory exposure.

Core Questions:
- Does this comply?
- Are contracts protective?
- Is data handled properly?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Compliance documentation
- Legal frameworks
- Regulatory alignment references

Regional Note:
Highly relevant in Saudi, UAE, and financial sectors.

---

### 6. Influencer / Internal Advocate

Typical Titles:
- Senior Specialist
- Consultant
- External Advisor
- Broker (Insurance)
- Agency Partner

Primary Concern:
Reputation and credibility.

Core Questions:
- Will recommending this damage my credibility?
- Does this align with industry best practice?

Touchpoint Priorities:
- Peer endorsement
- Industry validation
- Benchmark reports

Bridge Requirements:
Education → Validation must show independent support.

## 4. Influence vs Authority Mapping Template

Each account must be mapped using:

- Role
- Formal Authority Level (High / Medium / Low)
- Informal Influence Level (High / Medium / Low)
- Risk Sensitivity (Low / Moderate / High)
- Intent Stage
- Required Touchpoint Type
- Required Bridge Type

If no mapping exists,
strategy is considered incomplete.

---

## 5. Committee Complexity Levels

Level 1:
Single decision maker (SMEs)

Level 2:
2–3 stakeholders (Mid-market)

Level 3:
Multi-layer evaluation (Enterprise)

Level 4:
Enterprise + compliance + external advisors (Regulated / Government)

Bridge density increases with complexity level.

---

## 6. Regional Behavioral Notes

Egypt:
Relationship-driven acceleration.
Human touchpoints heavily influence speed.

Saudi Arabia:
Authority structures matter.
Formal hierarchy impacts progression.

UAE:
Faster decision cycles but strong compliance checks.

Cross-MENA Enterprise:
Documentation and references carry weight.

---

## 7. AI Agent Execution Logic

For each strategic account:

Step 1: Identify committee roles.
Step 2: Map authority vs influence.
Step 3: Assign touchpoint type per role.
Step 4: Assign bridge type between stages.
Step 5: Validate psychological progression alignment.

If one key authority role lacks:
- Validation
- Human exposure
- Risk reassurance

Flag as vulnerability.

## 8. Strategic Guardrail

Never design content "for the company".

Design content per role.

A strategy targeting a brand logo
without committee mapping
is structurally unstable.
