# Touchpoint Architecture Engine
Version: 1.0
Region Focus: Egypt – GCC – MENA
Depends On: 03_intent_mapping_framework.md

---

## 1. Purpose

This engine transforms mapped intent into a structured ecosystem of touchpoints.

Not campaigns.
Not funnels.
Not random channels.

A controlled sequence of psychological and strategic contact moments.

Touchpoints are decision-shaping events.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Touchpoints are designed around decision progression, not marketing activity.

The buyer does not move through channels.

The buyer moves through mental states.

Touchpoint architecture must map to state transitions.

---

## 3. The 5 Touchpoint Categories

Every strategy must distribute touchpoints across these five categories.

No category can be missing.

---

### 1. Presence Touchpoints

Objective:
Establish existence and authority.

Buyer Question:
"Who are they and are they credible?"

Examples:
- SEO dominance on strategic keywords
- AI search previews
- LinkedIn thought leadership
- Industry media features
- Executive interviews
- Event participation

Failure Risk:
If missing, the buyer will not proceed to evaluation.

---

### 2. Education Touchpoints

Objective:
Clarify the problem and elevate strategic understanding.

Buyer Question:
"Do they understand my problem deeply?"

Examples:
- Whitepapers
- Insight reports
- Deep-dive articles
- Executive briefs
- Market breakdown content
- Webinars

Failure Risk:
If weak, the buyer remains skeptical.

---

### 3. Validation Touchpoints

Objective:
Provide proof and reduce perceived risk.

Buyer Question:
"Has this worked for companies like mine?"

Examples:
- Case studies (local market preferred in GCC)
- Testimonials
- Industry awards
- Certifications
- Client logos
- Data-backed outcomes

Failure Risk:
Without validation, high-ticket B2B deals stall.

---

### 4. Human Touchpoints

Objective:
Convert brand trust into interpersonal trust.

Buyer Question:
"Who will I actually deal with?"

Examples:
- Executive commentary
- Direct LinkedIn engagement
- Speaking at events
- Personalized insights
- Strategic roundtables
- Behind-the-scenes leadership content

Failure Risk:
In MENA markets, absence of human trust delays decisions.

---

### 5. Conversion Touchpoints

Objective:
Make next step safe and structured.

Buyer Question:
"What happens if I engage?"

Examples:
- Demo process explanation
- Transparent onboarding steps
- Risk-reduction guarantees
- Pilot programs
- Structured proposal documents

Failure Risk:
Without clarity, fear blocks decision.

---

## 4. Touchpoint Sequencing Logic

Touchpoints must follow this transition model:

Presence → Education → Validation → Human → Conversion

However, buyers may move back and forth.

Architecture must allow non-linear journeys.

Never assume a straight line.

---

## 5. Intent-Aligned Weight Distribution

Touchpoint intensity depends on primary intent.

Example:

If Primary Intent = Trust Intent:
Heavy weight on Presence and Validation.

If Primary Intent = Conversion Efficiency:
Heavy weight on Human and Conversion.

If Primary Intent = Market Entry:
Heavy weight on Presence and Education.

The AI Agent must adjust distribution accordingly.

---

## 6. Committee-Based Mapping

Touchpoints must align with buyer roles.

Economic Buyer:
Needs validation and risk clarity.

Technical Buyer:
Needs education and documentation.

Executive Sponsor:
Needs presence and authority.

End User:
Needs usability reassurance.

Each touchpoint must declare target role.

---

## 7. Regional Adaptation Rules

Egypt:
Stronger Human touchpoints.
Referral amplification.
Less formal documentation.

GCC:
Formal validation heavy.
Executive alignment mandatory.
Risk mitigation emphasized.

MENA Enterprise:
Institutional credibility.
Cross-border reputation.
Structured documentation required.

Architecture must reflect regional behavior.

---

## 8. Architecture Output Format

The AI Agent must produce:

1. Primary Intent
2. Touchpoint Category Map
3. Role Mapping
4. Sequencing Logic
5. Regional Adjustment Notes
6. Risk Gaps (if any category is weak)

No vague strategy statements allowed.

All touchpoints must have:

- Objective
- Target Role
- Psychological State
- Strategic Purpose

---

## 9. Strategic Guardrail

If more than 40% of proposed actions are channel-driven instead of decision-driven:

Stop and refactor.

This is not a content calendar.

This is decision engineering.

---

## 10. Final Principle

Touchpoints are not marketing noise.

They are structured psychological reinforcement mechanisms.

The objective is not attention.

The objective is controlled decision progression.

End of Engine.
