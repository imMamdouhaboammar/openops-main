## 1. Purpose

Not all content persuades all roles.

In high-ticket B2B environments, decisions are made by committees — not individuals.

Each role evaluates the same offer through a different risk lens.

This system defines:

- The core mindset of each role
- Their primary fears
- Their desired outcomes
- The type of content that moves them forward
- The signals that indicate engagement

The goal is orchestration, not broadcasting.

---

## 2. Core Buying Roles (Universal Model)

Across Egypt, GCC and MENA markets, these roles consistently appear:

1) Strategic Sponsor (CEO / GM / Business Head)
2) Financial Authority (CFO / Finance Director)
3) Technical Authority (CTO / IT Director / Ops Head)
4) Functional Owner (Marketing Director / HR Director / Risk Head)
5) Risk & Compliance Gatekeeper
6) Procurement
7) Internal Champion

Each requires a different persuasion path.

# 3. Strategic Sponsor Playbook

## Mindset
- Long-term impact
- Reputation risk
- Market positioning
- Competitive advantage

"Will this decision strengthen or expose us?"

# 11. Orchestration Rule

No campaign should:

- Target only one role
- Push identical messaging to all roles
- Ignore the internal political dimension

Strategic success requires:

Multi-role narrative alignment.

If the CEO is inspired, but the CTO is skeptical,
The deal slows.

If the CFO approves, but compliance blocks,
The deal dies.

---

# 12. AI Agent Mandates

When generating content strategy:

1) Identify all relevant roles
2) Map intent stage per role
3) Assign Authority / Trust / Bridge content accordingly
4) Ensure sequence coherence
5) Avoid role-message conflict
6) Generate measurable engagement signals

Agent must reject:
- Single-role funnel strategies
- Generic persona assumptions
- Channel-based targeting without role clarity
