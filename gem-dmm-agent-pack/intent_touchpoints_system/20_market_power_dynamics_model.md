# Market Power Dynamics Model
Version: 1.0
Region Scope: Egypt – GCC – MENA
Applies To: B2B, Enterprise, Government, Regulated Markets, High-Ticket Services

---

## 1. Purpose

This model identifies:

- Who truly holds power in the market.
- What type of power they control.
- How that power shapes perception and decision-making.
- Where structural advantages exist.
- Where asymmetric moves are possible.

This is not competitor analysis.
This is power structure analysis.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Markets are not controlled by visibility alone.

Markets are shaped by:
- Capital
- Regulation
- Relationships
- Narrative
- Technology Control
- Distribution Control
- Institutional Trust

The strongest brand is not always the most powerful actor.

Power is multidimensional.

---

## 3. The 7 Power Axes

For every major player in the market,
map them across these axes:

1. Capital Power
2. Regulatory Power
3. Relationship Power
4. Narrative Power
5. Technological Power
6. Distribution Power
7. Institutional Trust Power

---

## 4. Capital Power

Questions:
- Who can outspend others sustainably?
- Who can survive long sales cycles?
- Who can absorb losses to gain dominance?

In GCC and MENA:
Capital often creates artificial leadership perception.

Score:
Low / Moderate / Strong / Dominant

---

## 5. Regulatory Power

Critical in:
- Insurance
- Fintech
- Healthcare
- Government projects
- Telecom
- Enterprise SaaS

Questions:
- Who has early regulator alignment?
- Who influences compliance standards?
- Who participates in policy discussions?
- Who is “default approved”?

Regulatory power can eliminate competitors without marketing.

---

## 6. Relationship Power

In MENA markets, relationships are structural.

Map:
- Government relationships
- Industry board memberships
- Enterprise partnerships
- Family-business networks
- Local sponsor leverage

Relationship power reduces friction in procurement.

---

## 7. Narrative Power

Who defines:
- What the problem is?
- What “modern” means?
- What “safe” means?
- What “premium” means?

If a competitor defines industry language,
they own the frame.

Frame ownership = cognitive power.

---

## 8. Technological Power

Questions:
- Who owns core infrastructure?
- Who controls proprietary data?
- Who integrates deeply into ecosystems?
- Who has switching cost advantage?

Technology can create lock-in beyond marketing.

---

## 9. Distribution Power

Distribution power includes:
- Channel partnerships
- Enterprise contracts
- Platform integrations
- Sales force scale
- Geographic presence

A competitor with distribution dominance
may win by access, not persuasion.

---

## 10. Institutional Trust Power

This is extremely critical in GCC.

Questions:
- Who feels safer?
- Who appears established?
- Who looks government-aligned?
- Who appears internationally recognized?

Perceived safety can override technical superiority.

---

## 11. Power Mapping Matrix

For each major player:

| Player | Capital | Regulation | Relationships | Narrative | Technology | Distribution | Trust | Overall Power Type |

Classify:
- Structural Dominator
- Regulatory Gatekeeper
- Narrative Shaper
- Relationship Network Controller
- Capital Heavyweight
- Technology Lock-in Player
- Perception Giant

---

## 12. Power Imbalance Detection

The Agent must detect:

- Where one axis is overly concentrated.
- Where hidden players exist.
- Where a small player has high narrative power.
- Where incumbents are vulnerable.

Example:
Large capital player but weak narrative power.
Opportunity: Thought leadership takeover.

---

## 13. Strategic Implications

After mapping, the Agent must answer:

1. Where is the real battlefield?
2. Which axis must we compete on?
3. Which axis must we avoid?
4. Where can asymmetric leverage be applied?
5. Where can we build alternative power?

Do not fight capital with capital.
Do not fight regulation without alignment.
Do not fight trust without proof.

---

## 14. Asymmetric Strategy Rule

If competitor dominates:
- Capital → Compete on agility and specialization.
- Regulation → Partner or align.
- Relationships → Build niche coalitions.
- Narrative → Reframe conversation.
- Technology → Simplify and de-risk.
- Distribution → Enter via underserved segments.
- Trust → Borrow trust via endorsements.

---

## 15. Completion Condition

The model is complete when:

- You know who truly holds influence.
- You understand how decisions are shaped.
- You identify structural advantages.
- You design strategy around power reality,
  not surface perception.

If your strategy ignores power dynamics,
it is fragile.

End of Framework.
