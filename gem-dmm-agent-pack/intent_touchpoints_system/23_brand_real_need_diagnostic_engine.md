# Brand Real Need Diagnostic Engine
Version: 1.0
Region Context: Egypt, GCC, MENA
Purpose: Identify the true strategic barrier preventing brand growth before prescribing tactics.

---

## 1. Core Principle

No brand needs “more content.”

Every brand needs to remove a specific resistance inside the market.

This engine exists to diagnose:
- What is actually blocking growth?
- Where is decision friction happening?
- What perception gap must be fixed first?

Tactics must always follow diagnosis.
Never the other way around.

---

## 2. The Diagnostic Hierarchy

The AI Agent must analyze in this order:

Step 1: Market Perception Reality
Step 2: Decision Friction Location
Step 3: Power Position vs Competitors
Step 4: Internal Capability Constraint
Step 5: Strategic Stage of the Brand

Only after these 5 layers are clear can a strategy be built.

---

## 3. Market Perception Scan

Ask:

1. Is the brand unknown?
2. Is the brand known but not trusted?
3. Is the brand trusted but not differentiated?
4. Is the brand visible but not preferred?
5. Is the brand strong but limited to a niche?

Output Format:

- Current Market Perception:
- Desired Market Perception:
- Perception Gap:

Example (B2B Cybersecurity, KSA):
Current: Known vendor among IT teams.
Desired: Strategic security partner for executive leadership.
Gap: Authority at board-level decision making.

---

## 5. Brand Real Need Archetypes

The system must classify the brand into one of these primary archetypes:

1. Authority Gap
   Brand is capable but lacks recognition.
   Prescription: Authority-building strategy.

2. Trust Gap
   Brand is visible but risky.
   Prescription: Validation-heavy strategy.

3. Positioning Blur
   Brand message is unclear or generic.
   Prescription: Narrative restructuring.

4. Access Problem
   Brand not reaching decision makers.
   Prescription: Intent capture + ecosystem mapping.

5. Conversion Friction
   Engagement exists but decisions stall.
   Prescription: Bridge strategy + de-risking assets.

6. Competitive Compression
   Brand stuck in crowded red ocean.
   Prescription: Differentiation warfare strategy.

7. Scaling Ceiling
   Brand saturated in current segment.
   Prescription: Market expansion or verticalization.

---

## 6. Internal Constraint Scan

Sometimes the problem is not market-facing.

Check:

- Sales capability gap?
- Weak case studies?
- Poor onboarding experience?
- Misaligned pricing model?
- Lack of vertical specialization?

If internal constraint > market constraint,
Strategy must prioritize internal enablement first.

---

## 7. Strategic Stage Identification

Identify stage:

1. Market Entry
2. Market Establishment
3. Competitive Expansion
4. Vertical Domination
5. Regional Expansion
6. Brand Repositioning

Each stage requires a different strategic architecture.

---

## 8. Prescription Framework

After classification, generate:

- Core Strategic Objective
- Required Perception Shift
- Primary Strategic Lever
- Supporting Tactical Families

Example:

Archetype: Trust Gap
Objective: Reduce perceived risk in enterprise deals.
Lever: Validation concentration.
Tactical Families:
- Case studies
- Executive testimonials
- Process transparency content
- Pilot programs

---

## 9. Tactical Alignment Rule

The AI Agent must reject tactics that do not directly serve the Real Need.

Example:

If Real Need = Authority Gap,
Reject:
- Heavy discount campaigns
- Viral social content unrelated to expertise

Accept:
- Industry reports
- Thought leadership
- Conference speaking
- SEO category ownership

---

## 10. Output Structure for Strategy Engine

Final diagnostic output must include:

- Real Need Classification
- Evidence Summary
- Strategic Risk Level (Low / Medium / High)
- Core Strategic Direction
- 90-Day Focus Priority

No content calendar is allowed before this step is complete.

---

## 11. Strategic Warning

Most failed strategies fail because:

- Tactics were chosen before diagnosis.
- Symptoms were treated instead of root cause.
- Budget allocation replaced thinking.

This engine exists to prevent that.
