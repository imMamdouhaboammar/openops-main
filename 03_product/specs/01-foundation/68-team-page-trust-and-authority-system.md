# Spec 68: Team Page Trust & Authority System
## Institutional Team Page for Trust, SEO, AEO & Internal Authority Flow

---

## Purpose

Create a **Team Page** that:

- Builds institutional trust (especially for B2G and enterprise audiences)
- Demonstrates depth of expertise without personal branding noise
- Supports SEO, AEO, and AI trust signals
- Acts as an internal authority node that reinforces services, content, and credibility

This is not an "About Us gallery".
This is a **trust infrastructure component**.

---

## Core Philosophy

The Team Page exists to answer one question:

"Who is capable of executing this responsibly?"

Not:
- Who is famous
- Who looks good
- Who talks the loudest

---

## Page Role in the IA

The Team Page is:
- A **trust anchor**
- A **supporting authority page**
- A **contextual reinforcement layer**

It does NOT:
- Compete with service pages
- Act as a sales page
- Replace portfolio proof

---

## Page URL & Placement

Recommended URL:

/team

Placement rules:
- Linked from:
  - About page
  - Footer (Trust section)
  - Service pages (contextual only)
- Not a primary nav CTA

---

## Content Structure

### 1. Page Introduction

Purpose:
- Explain how the team is structured
- Emphasize institutional capability over individuals

Content rules:
- Neutral tone
- Focus on disciplines, not personalities

Example angles:
- Multidisciplinary structure
- Government-grade execution readiness
- Clear accountability layers

---

### 2. Team Structure Blocks (Mandatory)

Team must be grouped by **function**, not ego.

Example groups:
- Strategy & Planning
- Event Operations & Execution
- Media & Production
- Crisis & Risk Management
- Digital & Technology
- Partnerships & Government Liaison

Each group includes:
- Role description
- Responsibility scope
- How it supports services

---

### 3. Individual Profiles (Controlled Exposure)

Each profile includes:
- Name
- Role
- Functional expertise
- Years of experience range (not exact dates)
- Domains worked in (government, enterprise, public)

Optional:
- Certifications
- Regulatory exposure
- Languages

Strict exclusions:
- Personal bios
- Motivational language
- Social links unless relevant

---

## SEO & AEO Optimization

### Target Queries

- "event management team Saudi Arabia"
- "government event experts Saudi"
- "institutional event management company team"

### On-page rules

- Clear H2 and H3 hierarchy
- Descriptive role-based headings
- No keyword stuffing

---

## Schema & Structured Data

Required schema:
- Organization
- Person (limited fields)
- BreadcrumbList

Rules:
- No exaggerated titles
- No fake credentials
- Consistent entity naming across site

---

## Internal Linking Strategy

### Incoming Links

Team page should receive links from:
- About page
- Pillar pages (credibility references)
- Service pages (execution responsibility sections)

---

### Outgoing Links (Highly Controlled)

Team page may link to:
- Core services (contextual)
- Portfolio page (proof handoff)

Never link to:
- Blog posts
- Marketing content
- CTAs

---

## Service Page Integration

Service pages must reference the team **indirectly**:

Examples:
- "Executed by a multidisciplinary team"
- "Supported by specialists in X and Y"

Links go to:
- Team page section anchors (not individual profiles)

---

## AI Trust & Citation Signals

Team page supports:
- AI confidence scoring
- Source credibility
- Answer trustworthiness

Requirements:
- Factual language
- Verifiable experience
- No exaggerated claims

---

## Design & UX Rules

- Clean, institutional layout
- No animations that distract
- Optional subtle motion for hierarchy only
- Accessible contrast and typography

---

## Update & Governance Rules

- Team updates trigger:
  - About page review
  - Service page trust sections review

- No ad-hoc edits
- Changes require review for consistency

---

## Failure Patterns to Avoid

- Turning team page into LinkedIn wall
- Overexposing individuals
- Making it salesy
- Orphaning the page from services

## Final Rule

If a team member cannot be tied to execution responsibility,
they do not appear on this page.

Trust is built by structure, not spotlight.
