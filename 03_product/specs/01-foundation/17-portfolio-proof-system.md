## 1. WHY THIS SPEC EXISTS

In B2G and enterprise environments:
A portfolio is not inspiration.
A portfolio is **evidence**.

Images alone do not convince:
- Procurement
- Decision committees
- Executive reviewers

This spec turns the portfolio into a **structured proof system**, not a gallery.

---

## 2. PORTFOLIO PHILOSOPHY

Every case study must answer clearly:
- What was the problem?
- What did "$BrandNameArabic$" do?
- How was it executed?
- What changed because of it?

If a case cannot answer these, it does not belong.

---

## 3. CASE STUDY DATA MODEL (MANDATORY)

Each portfolio case must include the following blocks in this exact order:

1. Context & Client Type
2. The Problem
3. "$BrandNameArabic$" Role
4. The Solution
5. Deliverables
6. Impact & Results (if available)
7. Visual Evidence

No reordering without approval.

---

## 4. CONTEXT & CLIENT TYPE

Purpose:
Anchor the case in a real-world scenario.

Must include:
- Industry or sector (e.g., Government, Events, Corporate)
- Event or project type
- Scope (local, national, international)
- Year

No confidential client names if restricted.

---

## 5. THE PROBLEM

Rules:
- Describe the challenge, not the brief
- Focus on constraints and risks
- Avoid emotional or marketing language

Example directions:
- Timeline pressure
- Audience complexity
- Scale or sensitivity
- Media risk
- Technical limitations

---

## 6. "$BrandNameArabic$" ROLE

Must clarify:
- Lead role or supporting role
- Strategic vs execution responsibility
- Decision-making authority level

This section prevents credit ambiguity.

---

## 7. THE SOLUTION

Describe:
- Strategic approach
- Creative direction
- Operational execution
- Technologies or methods used

Focus on **how**, not adjectives.

---

## 8. DELIVERABLES

Mandatory bullet list format.

Examples:
- Event concept & narrative
- Visual identity
- Stage design
- Media production
- Live broadcast
- Digital amplification
- Media center management

No vague phrasing.

---

## 9. IMPACT & RESULTS (OPTIONAL BUT ENCOURAGED)

If data is available:
- Attendance numbers
- Reach
- Engagement
- Media mentions
- Operational KPIs

Rules:
- Be honest
- Approximate if needed
- Never exaggerate

If no data exists:
- State clearly that results are qualitative

---

## 10. VISUAL EVIDENCE SYSTEM

### Mandatory Visual Types (at least one)

- Hero image
- Gallery (execution shots)
- Before / After (when applicable)
- Short recap video (optional)

---

### Gallery Rules

- No decorative images
- Every image must show execution reality
- No repeated angles
- Mobile-friendly swipe behavior

---

## 11. IMAGE & MEDIA GOVERNANCE

- Images must follow Media Pipeline spec
- Naming conventions enforced
- Alt text required
- No low-resolution assets
- No stock photos

---

## 12. PORTFOLIO FILTERING & DISCOVERY

Cases must be filterable by:
- Service
- Industry
- Year
- Scope

Empty states must follow Error & Empty State spec.

---

## 13. SEO & AEO STRUCTURE

Each case must:
- Be indexable
- Have unique title & description
- Answer “what was done” clearly
- Avoid buzzwords

Case studies are **authority content**, not marketing copy.

---

## 14. CONTENT SOURCE OF TRUTH

Each case lives in:

```

/Core/portfolio/case-slug.md

```

Media referenced explicitly.
No inline content in JSX.

---

## 15. GOVERNANCE & APPROVAL

- Case studies require approval before publish
- Sensitive projects reviewed carefully
- No retroactive edits without documentation

---

## 16. PROCUREMENT & ENTERPRISE VALUE

This system:
- Reduces procurement friction
- Supports technical evaluation
- Enables internal client justification
- Differentiates "$BrandNameArabic$" from visual-only agencies
